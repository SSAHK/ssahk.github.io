Surgical Taster Day 2015

Professor Ng Chi Fai Anthony

After completing his medical education at CUHK, Professor Ng Chi Fai Anthony joined the Department of Surgery at Prince of Wales Hospital as a surgical trainee. He completed his training in Urology in 2001. With a keen interest in the endourology, he went to Scotland for a year to gain extensive experience in endo-laparoscopic surgery, robotic surgery, uro-oncology and stone management.

Currently, Professor Ng is a professor of Division of Urology, Department of Surgery, CUHK and the Co-director of Youth Urological Treatment Centre. His main interests include urolithiasis, prostate diseases and Men's Health. He also received the Outstanding Young Urologist Award in 2010 by the Asian Congress of Urology as recognition to his work in the field.

Dr. Yiu Ming Kwong

In 1985, Dr. Yiu graduated from HKU. He joined the Department of Surgery in the Prince of Margaret Hospital since 1987 and sub-specialised in Urology after acquiring his Fellowship from the Royal College of Surgeons of Edinburgh in 1989.

Dr. Yiu is now a Consultant and Division Chief of Urology at HKU. He is also a co-opted Council Member of the Hong Kong Society of Transplantation, and former President of the Hong Kong Urological Association. He has also served as a Program Director of the Urology Board of the College of Surgeons of Hong Kong. His main subspecialty interests include laparoscopic urology, paediatric urology and renal transplant surgery.

Photo Credit: Sing Pao

Dr. Kwok Po-Yin Samuel

After graduation from the University of Hong Kong in 1983, Dr. Kwok Po-Yin Samuel has worked as a Specialist in General Surgery in the Prince of Wales Hospital, before acting as the Chief of Service in the United Christian Hospital for almost 20 years. He has made a huge contribution in promoting laparoscopic surgery in Hong Kong, where he founded the Minimally Invasive Surgery Development Centre in UCH.

Dr. Kwok moved from public to private sector since 2002, he founded the Minimally Invasive Surgery Center in Hong Kong Sanatorium & Hospital, after which he has served as the director of Endoscopy and Minimally Invasive Surgery Education Center in Hong Kong Adventist Hospital. He is currently a Consultant Surgeon in the Pedder Clinic in Hong Kong.

Dr. Kwok is now the President of Association of Private Medical Specialists of Hong Kong, past president of the College of Surgeons of Hong Kong (2004-2007) and the past president of the Hong Kong Society For Coloproctology.

Mr. Pasu Ng

Previously worked as a designer, Mr. Pasu Ng was determined to pursue a different career path. He is now an embalmer at the Chinese University of Hong Kong. His duties include body embalming, specimen production and the operation of teaching at the dissection laboratory.

Despite his busy schedule, Mr. Ng is actively involved in the promotion and operation of the Body Donation Programme “Silent Teachers”. In addition to his contribution in medical school, he values every opportunity to spread positive messages about life and death, in which he created a Life and Death Education group on Facebook with Rev. Chris Wong. The group has attracted over 3,000 members.
